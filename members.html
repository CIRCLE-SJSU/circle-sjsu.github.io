CIRCLE

Members

The CIRCLE team combines deep scholarly expertise across several disciplines relevant to responsible computing with leadership and experience in equity, diversity, inclusion, and social justice pedagogy and education. The core team consists of researchers in law and archival science (Darra Hofman), AI and machine learning (Souvick Ghosh), cultural competence and legal informatics (Michele Villagran), computer vision and Human-Computer Interaction (Nada Attar), and Critical Race and Ethnic Studies (Saugher Nojan).

nada.attar@sjsu.edu

Biography
Attar’s work focuses on Computer Vision and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). Her research in HCI can play a critical role in teaching the innovation of user interface design and implementation of high-performance applications, while considering the ethics in computer science. For example, her recent publication on ACM MuC22 focused on ethnocentric and gender biases and how it can be applied in this context via the use of human-computer interface. This recent study offers quantitative insight into measuring biases, thoughtful interaction with art as stimulus. The study focused on reducing implicit gender bias towards females without the need for explicit coaching. Attar’s research in Computer Vision is related to Facial Expression Recognition (FER), where racial and gender bias could be embedded in the datasets. Her current research focuses on building models to measure bias in computer vision datasets. This research could mainly help in diversity and inclusion (D&I) curriculum design in the teaching setting which allows students to build sophisticated tools for testing datasets and evaluating models in Computer Vision.

Biography
Dr. Souvick “Vic” Ghosh is an Assistant Professor at the School of Information at San Jose State University. He is the Academic Coordinator for the undergraduate degree program (BS) in Information Science and Data Analytics and the Director of the Intelligent Conversational Agents and Neural Networks Lab (ICANN) at SJSU. Ghosh also serves as the AI Consultant for the InterPARES Trust AI and the Chair for the ASIS&T IDEA Artificial Intelligence Institute. Prior to joining SJSU, he completed his Ph.D. in Information Science from Rutgers University in 2020. He is a mixed methods researcher who combines interdisciplinary concepts from artificial intelligence, machine learning, and human-computer interaction (HCI) to improve voice-based assistants and design systems for the social good. He developed automated AI solutions for complex socio-technical problems such as cyberbullying, fake news, community question-answering, and PII (personally identifiable information) disclosure. Of particular use to this project is Ghosh’s research using a mixed-methods approach to combine concepts from AI and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) to improve voice-based assistants.

Biography
Hofman’s work focuses on the intersection of law and archival science to extend understandings of how emerging information technologies function in broader sociolegal systems, with a particular emphasis on privacy and marginalized people. Hofman’s publications (reach across boundaries, engaging archivists, lawyers, and computer scientists to examine questions of rights, governance, and broader impacts of emerging technologies from a humanistic perspective, with an emphasis on questions of trustworthiness and user needs.

Biography
Nojan’s work examines how societal inequities are formed and sustained in relation to racial formations, religion, immigration, civic and political participation, and education. Her expertise in sociology generally, and in the intersectional nature of systems of oppression, provide a critical lens for educating students in responsible computing, and offers a social science foundation to complement the more humanistic and technical lens of other team members.

Biography
Villagran comes to the project with a unique combination of expertise in cultural competence and intelligence, and legal informatics. Building out of this research focus, Villagran frequently consults in diversity, equity and inclusion, and cultural intelligence, and is a recognized leader in the domain, as evidence by her keynotes talks to Pennsylvania Library Association, Minnesota Library Association, Amigos Library Services, Arizona Library Association, and California Dialysis Council. Her prior research focused on the phenomena of cultural intelligence within different segments: law firm libraries, special libraries including corporate, medical, law; state-specific libraries, and the application of cultural within library and information science. Villagran also has an important body of work on marginalized populations: immigrant communities, trans* people, and first-generation students (Bernier, et al, 2020). Villagran, Hofman and Ghosh together have a scholarly project, Legally, Algorithmically, and Culturally Aware Systems of Accountability (LACASA), for which Villagran and Hofman obtained initial funding from the Association for Library and Information Science Education, examining digital privacy for marginalized communities, including queer communities.

Dr. Villagran chairs, and Drs. Ghosh and Hofman are members of, the Academic Committee on Equity, Diversity, Inclusion, and Social Justice for SJSU’s College of Professional and Global Education. During their tenure on that committee, they have initiated a project on Anti-Oppressive Pedagogy that has produced, so far, a literature review, webinar, and training on anti-oppressive pedagogy within library and information science and data science and a training program for college faculty on implementing anti-oppressive pedagogy within the classroom; the CIRCLE team will build on this experience.
